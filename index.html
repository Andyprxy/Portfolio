Rail Manufacturing Optimization

Location: Gibela Rail Transport Consortium | Methodology: DMAIC
64%Cycle Time Reduction
69%Labor Cost Savings
+27Ergo Score Points
100%Safety Compliance

1. Define: The Bottleneck

A critical station in the Car Body Shell assembly line faced a severe inefficiency. Operators were manually lifting 30kg steel bridging ramps to connect the platform to the train. This manual process required two operators and resulted in high variability (Setup times fluctuating between 5 and 30 minutes), disrupting the Takt time.

2. Analyze: Data-Driven Root Cause

I utilized MODAPTS (Modular Arrangement of Predetermined Time Standards) to quantify human motion. The analysis revealed that "Waiting for Assistance" and "Heavy Lifting" (Code L6) accounted for 80% of process waste.

  • Fishbone Analysis: Confirmed root causes as Method (Manual Lifting) and Machine (Rigid Ramp Design).
  • Risk Assessment: The process had an initial Ergonomic Score of 45% (High Risk).

3. Improve: Engineering Design

I conceptualized and engineered a "Rollable Guide System" to replace vertical lifting with a horizontal sliding mechanism.

  • CAD Design: Designed a custom frame using Aluminum Square Tubing in SolidWorks.
  • Simulation: Conducted FEA (Finite Element Analysis) to simulate load-bearing capacity (250kg) and ensure structural integrity.
  • Fabrication: Utilized reclaimed materials from decommissioned jigs to minimize CAPEX costs.
SolidWorks FEA Simulation Lean Manufacturing

4. Control: Standardization

Authored a new Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) and integrated it into the Visual Management board. The new process stabilized the cycle time to consistently under 5 minutes, permanently eliminating the bottleneck.

The Innovation Challenge

Rural communities struggle with water accessibility. Existing solutions lack inclusivity for the elderly and disabled due to heavy weight and awkward pouring mechanisms. We redesigned the water roller to be inclusive and sustainable using a 3-Size System (30L, 45L, 90L) and a "Push/Pull" ergonomic handle.

Design for Manufacturing (DFM)

We engineered the production process for scalability. Using SolidWorks, we optimized the mold flow for an automated ARBURG injection molding machine.

  • Material Selection: Recycled HDPE & PP (Aligning with SDG 13: Climate Action).
  • Output: Calculated peak production rate of 382 units/hour with a 2-minute cycle time.
